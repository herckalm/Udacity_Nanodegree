Data Exploration of the performance of students in Mathematics and Science, based on the results of the PISA 2012 test¶

by (Iraklis Kalamas)¶

Quoting from the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development web site (http://www.oecd.org/pisa/):

"The Programme for International Student Assessment (PISA) is the OECD's Programme for International Student Assessment. PISA measures 15-year-olds ability to use their reading, mathematics and science knowledge and skills to meet real-life challenges. More specifically PISA 2012 is the programmes 5th survey. It assessed the competencies of 15-year-olds in reading, mathematics and science (with a focus on mathematics) in 65 countries and economies. In 44 of those countries and economies about 85 000 students also took part in an optional assessment of creative problem solving; and in 18 countries and economies, students were assessed in financial literacy."

Now I'm ready to answer the following questions¶

What is the structure of your dataset?¶

The final data set contains 10 features and 347783 observations. This is a part of the original data, sufficient for my analysis.

What is/are the main feature(s) of interest in your dataset?¶

My main interest is to find insights for the score of students in math and science in relation to their family structure.

What features in the dataset do you think will help support your investigation into your feature(s) of interest?¶

I'll need the scores of students in math and science and data from family structure such as the level of education of the parent(s), the presence or not of the parent(s) at home, if the presence of siblings affect the performance of students and the time that parent(s) spend with the student.

score_df = df.groupby(['Study_Time_With_Parent']).mean().reset_index()[['Study_Time_With_Parent','Mean_Score','Math_Score',
                                                                        'Science_Score']]

               value_vars=['Mean_Score','Math_Score', 'Science_Score'], 
               var_name="Score_Type", value_name='Score')

fig, ax = plt.subplots(1, 2, figsize=(25, 8))

sb.lineplot(ax=ax[0], data = data, x = 'Study_Time_With_Parent', y='Score', hue='Score_Type');
ax[0].set(title='How the scores affected by the parents help', xlabel='Hours', ylabel='Score')

sb.heatmap(df[['Mean_Score','Math_Score', 'Science_Score', 'Study_Time_With_Parent']].corr(), annot = True, fmt = '.3f',
           cmap = 'vlag_r', center = 0)
ax[1].set(title='Correlation between the scores and the time spent studying with parents')

plt.suptitle("Styduing with parents helps students or not?", y=0.98);
  • #### From the univariate exploration I reached the conclusion that it's very rare that students spend more than 5 hours sudying with their parent(s).
  • #### With that in mind I can see that:
    • #### when considering only number of hours between 0 and 5, there's a significant drop of about 70 points in the score, so studying with parents does not seems to be a factor of success
    • #### As the hours studing with parent grows the performance is not clearly affected, meaning that sometimes the performance affected positively and sometimes negatively (maybe some students have problems with math and science that parents sometimes can solve and sometimes can't due to academic background)
    • #### The heat map in right shows that there is a weak negative correlation between scores and study time with parent.
